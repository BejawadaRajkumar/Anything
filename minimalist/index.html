min·i·mal·ism

(noun)

The art of less.

Minimalism is the intentional promotion of the things we most value and the removal of everything that distracts us from it. It's not about deprivation; it's about purpose.

"Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." – Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

Core Principles:

  • Identify the essential.
  • Eliminate the rest.
  • Focus on quality, not quantity.
  • Create space for what matters.
